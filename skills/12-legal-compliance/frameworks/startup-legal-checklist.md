# Startup Legal Checklist — Stage by Stage

> **Purpose:** A comprehensive stage-by-stage legal checklist so founders know what needs to be done, in what order, and when. Use this to identify gaps and prioritize legal work.

---

> **LEGAL DISCLAIMER:** This checklist is educational only. It does not constitute legal advice and is not a substitute for qualified legal counsel. The items listed are common for US-based startups — your specific requirements will vary based on jurisdiction, business model, industry, and circumstances. **Consult a qualified startup attorney** to review and complete each stage. Laws and regulations change — some items on this list may be outdated for your situation.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Identify your current stage (Pre-Formation → Formed → Seed Round → Series A → Scaling)
2. Work through all items in your current stage
3. Preview the next stage so you know what's coming
4. Items marked ⚠️ are time-critical — missing them creates hard-to-fix problems
5. Items marked 🔄 are ongoing and must be maintained

---

## Stage 1: Pre-Formation

*Before you have a legal entity. Goal: make informed entity and structure decisions before filing.*

### Legal Prep
- [ ] Research entity types and select structure (see `templates/entity-selection-guide.md`)
- [ ] Confirm all founders and agree on equity split, vesting, and roles
- [ ] Have "hard conversation" checklist completed (`templates/founder-agreement.md`)
- [ ] Review prior employer agreements for all founders — IP claim risk?
- [ ] Select startup attorney (interview 2–3; get fee estimates)
- [ ] Confirm company name availability in target state and trademark screening (basic)

### Key Decisions
- [ ] Entity type: Delaware C-Corp (VC path) or LLC (other path)
- [ ] State of incorporation: Delaware (VC) or home state (no VC)
- [ ] Registered agent identified (required in state of incorporation)

## Stage 2: Formation and Setup

*Incorporating and building the legal foundation. Goal: clean cap table, IP assigned, and protection in place.*

### Corporate Formation
- [ ] File Certificate of Incorporation
- [ ] Adopt bylaws
- [ ] Hold organizational board meeting or execute written consent
  - [ ] Elect officers (CEO, Secretary, Treasurer)
  - [ ] Authorize initial share issuance
  - [ ] Adopt equity incentive plan (option pool)
- [ ] Obtain EIN from IRS
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] File foreign qualification in home state (if different from state of incorporation)

### Founder Documents
- [ ] Execute Stock Purchase Agreement for each founder
- [ ] Set vesting schedule (4-year / 1-year cliff standard)
- ⚠️ [ ] **File 83(b) election within 30 days of stock grant — HARD DEADLINE**
  - [ ] Prepare form
  - [ ] Mail certified mail to IRS
  - [ ] Retain certified mail receipt
  - [ ] Attach to personal tax return for that year
- [ ] Sign PIIA (Proprietary Information and Inventions Assignment) for each founder
- [ ] Sign founder agreement (roles, departure terms, non-solicitation)

### IP Protection
- [ ] IP assignment agreement for all pre-incorporation work
- [ ] PIIA confirms assignment of ongoing work to company
- [ ] Review prior employer IP agreements for each founder (attorney review)
- [ ] Contractor IP assignment agreements (for any contractors who built the product)
- [ ] Document trade secrets and implement reasonable secrecy measures
- [ ] Consider provisional patent if core invention is patent-eligible (ask attorney)

### Basic Business Setup
- [ ] Trademark search for company name and product name
- [ ] File trademark applications (consider early — process takes 12–18 months)
- [ ] Privacy policy and Terms of Service for any website or app
- [ ] NDA template for use with potential employees, contractors, partners

🔄 **Ongoing:** Maintain corporate records (minutes or consents for major decisions)

## Stage 3: First Employees and Contractors

*Bringing on team members. Goal: proper classification, IP assignment, and employment documentation.*

### Employment Documents
- [ ] Offer letter template (with at-will employment language)
- [ ] PIIA for every employee (same IP assignment as founders)
- [ ] Employee handbook (basic — policies, code of conduct)
- [ ] I-9 verification for every employee (required by federal law, regardless of company size)
- [ ] State and local registration for payroll tax withholding in each state with employees

### Worker Classification
- [ ] Formal classification of each worker (employee vs. contractor)
  - Factors: control, integration, equipment, permanency, exclusivity
  - If in doubt: default to employee; reclassification penalty risk is significant
  - California AB-5 and similar state laws have strict rules — mandatory attorney review if CA employees
- [ ] Contractor agreements with IP assignment for all 1099 contractors

### Compensation and Equity
- ⚠️ [ ] Obtain 409A valuation before first option grant
- [ ] Option grant board approvals (options must be approved by board, not just CEO)
- [ ] Stock option grant letters for each optionee (with vesting schedule, exercise price, expiration)
- [ ] Payroll setup (Gusto, Rippling, or similar — handles tax withholding and filings)

🔄 **Ongoing:**
- Renew 409A valuation annually or after material events
- Update option pool as grants are made
- I-9 reverification for employees with temporary work authorization

## Stage 4: Seed Financing

*Raising a seed round. Goal: execute financing cleanly, comply with securities law, maintain clean cap table.*

### Pre-Financing Prep
- [ ] Cap table cleanup (verify all shares are properly documented, no informal grants)
- [ ] IP audit (confirm all IP is assigned to company, no gaps)
- [ ] Board authorization for the financing
- [ ] Term sheet review (attorney reviews terms before signing — even seed stage)

### SAFE / Convertible Note Execution
- [ ] Select instrument type (SAFE vs. convertible note — see SKILL.md Frameworks)
- [ ] Model cap table impact of each instrument before signing
- [ ] Use standard documents (YC SAFE or NVCA convertible note templates)
- ⚠️ [ ] **File Form D with SEC within 15 days of first sale**
  - Available at SEC EDGAR
  - Required for Regulation D, Rule 506 exemption
  - Failure to file is a securities law violation
- [ ] Verify each investor's accredited investor status (questionnaire + documentation)
- [ ] State "Blue Sky" filings (some states require notice filings for exempt offerings — attorney confirms)
- [ ] Execute closing documents (SAFE agreements, closing checklist)

### Post-Closing
- [ ] Update cap table with new instruments
- [ ] Issue investor information rights letters (if negotiated)
- [ ] Data room setup for future due diligence
- [ ] Board observer rights (if any investor negotiated them)

🔄 **Ongoing:**
- Monthly investor updates (if committed in SAFE terms)
- Maintain accredited investor records for 5 years

## Stage 5: Series A (Priced Round)

*Institutional financing. Goal: clean, efficient due diligence and closing.*

### Pre-Series A Prep (6+ months before close)
- [ ] Financial statements: 2+ years (audited or reviewed — discuss requirement with lead investor)
- [ ] Cap table (attorney-confirmed fully diluted)
- [ ] Complete IP chain-of-title review
- [ ] Employment agreement updates (make sure all key employees have signed PIIAs)
- [ ] 409A current (< 12 months old)
- [ ] Option grants properly approved by board
- [ ] Corporate minutes and consents complete and organized

### Term Sheet and Due Diligence
- [ ] Attorney review of term sheet (critical — negotiate key terms: valuation, option pool, liquidation preference, pro-rata rights, information rights, board composition)
- [ ] Data room organized:
  - [ ] Corporate: cap table, governance docs, board minutes
  - [ ] IP: assignment agreements, patents, trademarks
  - [ ] Employment: key employee agreements, org chart
  - [ ] Contracts: customer contracts, vendor contracts, NDAs
  - [ ] Financial: audited financials, 3-year model, unit economics
  - [ ] Regulatory/compliance: applicable licenses, permits
- [ ] Background checks on founders (some lead investors conduct these)

### Closing
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation amendment (for Preferred Stock designation)
- [ ] Investor Rights Agreement (information rights, pro-rata rights, registration rights)
- [ ] Voting Agreement (board composition, drag-along rights)
- [ ] Right of First Refusal Agreement
- [ ] Certificate of Designation for Preferred Stock
- [ ] Board approval of all closing documents
- ⚠️ [ ] Form D amendment filed within 15 days of Series A closing
- [ ] State Blue Sky filings (attorney coordinates)
- [ ] Option pool increase (board approval)

### Post-Close
- [ ] Cap table updated with Series A Preferred Stock
- [ ] New board member(s) onboarded (board charter, D&O insurance)
- [ ] D&O insurance obtained (typically required by Series A investors)
- [ ] Investor update cadence formalized (quarterly board meetings, monthly updates)

## Stage 6: Scaling — Compliance Infrastructure

*Growing team, revenue, and complexity. Goal: proactive compliance before problems arise.*

### Employment and HR
- [ ] Employee handbook updated as team scales (beyond 10 employees, many state laws add requirements)
- [ ] Anti-harassment and anti-discrimination policies (required in most states at certain headcounts)
- [ ] FMLA compliance (required at 50+ employees)
- [ ] 401(k) plan (typically established at 20+ employees)
- [ ] Worker classification audit (annual)

### Data Privacy and Security
- [ ] GDPR compliance (if any EU users or customers — required regardless of company location)
  - Privacy policy in GDPR-compliant format
  - Data processing agreements with vendors
  - Consent mechanisms where required
  - Data subject rights processes
- [ ] CCPA compliance (if California residents are users — required at > $25M revenue, >50k users, or >50% data revenue)
- [ ] SOC 2 Type I (recommend when selling to enterprise customers — typically 6–12 months to achieve)
- [ ] Penetration testing (at least annual; required by most enterprise security questionnaires)
- [ ] Incident response plan (what to do if there's a data breach)

### Industry-Specific Compliance

**Healthcare (HIPAA):**
- [ ] Business Associate Agreements (BAA) with all vendors who access PHI
- [ ] HIPAA Security Rule compliance (administrative, physical, technical safeguards)
- [ ] Breach notification procedures

**Payments (PCI-DSS):**
- [ ] If processing card payments directly: PCI-DSS Level 4 self-assessment questionnaire
- [ ] If using Stripe/Braintree/etc.: verify you're in scope and complete SAQ

**Financial Services (FINRA, SEC):**
- [ ] Determine applicable regulatory framework based on activities
- [ ] Registration requirements vary significantly — attorney review is mandatory

**International Operations:**
- [ ] Local employment law compliance in each country
- [ ] VAT/GST registration where required
- [ ] Data localization requirements (some countries require data stored locally)

### Contracts and IP
🔄 **Ongoing:**
- [ ] Customer contract template updated annually (or after significant product changes)
- [ ] Vendor contract review for new material agreements (attorney review for contracts > $X threshold)
- [ ] Annual IP audit: any new inventions, designs, or trade secrets that should be protected?
- [ ] Trademark renewals (typically every 5–10 years depending on jurisdiction)

## Stage 7: Exit Preparation (M&A or IPO)

*Preparing for a liquidity event. Goal: no surprises in due diligence that reduce valuation or kill the deal.*

### Legal House-Cleaning
- [ ] Corporate records review: all minutes, consents, and resolutions from inception
- [ ] IP chain-of-title: every assignment documented; no gaps
- [ ] Option plan compliance: all grants board-approved, all 409As current
- [ ] Employment agreements: key employee retention agreements negotiated
- [ ] Material contracts: change of control provisions reviewed (some contracts have termination rights on acquisition)
- [ ] Litigation review: any pending or threatened claims disclosed and addressed

### Data Room (M&A)
- All items from Series A data room, updated
- 3 years of audited financial statements
- Current 409A valuation
- Complete cap table with all dilution scenarios modeled
- Any regulatory approvals or compliance certifications

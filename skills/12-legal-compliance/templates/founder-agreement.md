# Founder Agreement — Key Terms Checklist

> **Purpose:** Ensure all critical founder relationship terms are discussed and agreed before incorporation. This checklist drives the conversation — the actual agreements should be drafted by a startup attorney.

---

> **LEGAL DISCLAIMER:** This checklist is educational only. It does not constitute legal advice and should not be used as a substitute for attorney-drafted agreements. Founder agreements have significant legal, tax, and financial consequences. **Consult a qualified startup attorney** to draft, review, and finalize any founder agreements before signing. These conversations are best had before incorporating — they are much harder and more expensive to resolve after the fact.

---

## Why This Document Exists

Most cofounder disputes — including the ones that kill companies — stem from ambiguity about these topics. The time to resolve ambiguity is before you're building a product together, raising money, or have customers depending on you.

**The hard truth:** The probability that all cofounders remain with the company through exit is low. Plan for departures, disagreements, and life changes — not because you don't trust each other, but because explicit agreements protect the relationship better than implicit assumptions.

---

## Pre-Work: The Difficult Conversations

Before using this checklist, have these conversations out loud — not over email:

1. "What happens if one of us wants to leave in 6 months?"
2. "What happens if we raise money and investors want to push one of us out?"
3. "Who has final say when we disagree on strategy?"
4. "What if one of us has a personal financial emergency and needs to work elsewhere?"
5. "What if we fundamentally disagree on what market to pursue?"

If any of these conversations feel too uncomfortable to have, that discomfort is a signal — not a reason to avoid the conversation.

### 1.2 Vesting Schedule

**All founder shares should vest over time, regardless of how long founders have worked together.**

| Founder | Vesting Duration | Cliff | Monthly Vesting | Start Date |
|---------|-----------------|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| [Founder 1] | [4 years] | [1 year / none] | [After cliff: 1/36 per month] | [Date] |
| [Founder 2] | [4 years] | [1 year / none] | | |

**Custom vesting considerations:**

*If a founder joined after significant work was done:*
- Consider a shorter cliff (6 months) or partial initial vest to reflect prior contribution
- Document: "[Founder X] had already contributed [describe] before formal incorporation. Their vesting reflects [X months] credit for prior contribution."

*If founders have worked together for > 1 year before incorporating:*
- Some companies back-date the vesting start date to reflect actual time working on the project
- Others grant partial vesting at incorporation to reflect prior work
- Either approach requires attorney input; tax implications exist

**83(b) Election commitment:**
- [ ] All founders agree to file 83(b) election within 30 days of stock issuance
- [ ] Attorney is responsible for preparing 83(b) forms for all founders
- Acknowledgment: All founders understand missing the 30-day deadline cannot be corrected

**Note on single trigger (all unvested accelerates on acquisition, regardless of termination):** VCs generally oppose single trigger acceleration for any employee including founders, because it reduces incentives for founders to remain post-acquisition — which is often a key condition of the acquisition. ### 4.2 Involuntary Departure — Termination for Cause

*"Cause" should be defined precisely in the founder agreement to prevent disputes.*

**Cause includes (suggested list — attorney should define precisely):**
- Material breach of fiduciary duty
- Conviction of a felony
- Fraud, embezzlement, or theft from the company
- Willful misconduct materially harmful to the company
- Material violation of company policies after written notice and cure period

*In case of termination for cause:*
| Item | Agreement |
|------|-----------|
| Unvested shares | Forfeited immediately |
| Vested shares | [Company has right to repurchase at original price / No repurchase right] |

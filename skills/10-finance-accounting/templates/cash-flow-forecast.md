# 13-Week Rolling Cash Flow Forecast

> **Purpose:** Project your cash position week by week for the next 13 weeks. This is your earliest warning system — it reveals cash crises 8–10 weeks before they happen, giving you time to act.

---

> **Financial Disclaimer:** Cash flow projections are estimates based on assumptions. Actual results will vary. This template does not constitute financial advice. Consult a qualified accountant or financial advisor before making significant decisions based on this forecast.

*Note: This template is educational. Consult a qualified accountant for advice specific to your situation.*

---

## Why 13 Weeks?

- 13 weeks = 1 calendar quarter
- Payroll happens every 2 weeks (or twice a month) — a monthly view misses these timing effects
- Annual contracts paid upfront create large single-week inflows that distort monthly averages
- You need 8–10 weeks of lead time to meaningfully address a cash shortfall (cut costs, bridge, close deals faster)
- A monthly P&L shows what happened; a weekly cash flow forecast shows what's about to happen

**Update frequency:** Weekly. Every Monday, shift the window one week forward, enter actuals for the prior week, and reforecast.

## Red Flags

Auto-flag these conditions and take immediate action:

- [ ] **Any week's projected balance falls below safety threshold** → Take action 8 weeks before the trough, not when you're in it
- [ ] **Actual inflows < 70% of forecast two weeks in a row** → Revenue assumptions need revision; reforecast
- [ ] **Actual outflows > 120% of forecast** → Unplanned expenses; identify and categorize
- [ ] **End-of-period cash < 3 months of projected burn** → Fundraising or bridge conversations required

## Section 1: Pricing Strategy Selection

Three common strategies — only value-based pricing is recommended for new products. The others are included for context.

| Strategy | How it works | When appropriate | Primary risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cost-plus** | Price = COGS + desired margin % | Manufacturing, commodities, services with clear labor cost | Systematically underprices when customer value is much higher than cost |
| **Competitor-based** | Price = match or undercut competitor prices | Commoditized markets with price-sensitive buyers | Creates race to the bottom; assumes you and competitors have same value |
| **Value-based** | Price = fraction of economic value delivered to customer | B2B software, unique products, clear ROI use cases | Requires understanding customer economics |
| **Freemium** | Free basic tier + paid premium tier | PLG products with viral expansion, high volume required | Free tier must create genuine habit; otherwise free users never convert |

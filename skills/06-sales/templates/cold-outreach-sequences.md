# Cold Outreach Sequences

> Email and LinkedIn sequences with timing, copy frameworks, and performance benchmarks. These templates are starting points — personalize the first line of every message before sending.

---

## Sequence Performance Benchmarks

Before building sequences, understand what "good" looks like:

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Great |
|--------|------|---------|------|-------|
| Email open rate | < 20% | 20–30% | 30–45% | > 45% |
| Email reply rate | < 3% | 3–7% | 7–15% | > 15% |
| LinkedIn connection accept rate | < 20% | 20–35% | 35–50% | > 50% |
| Meeting booking rate | < 1% | 1–2% | 2–5% | > 5% |

**Rule:** Kill any sequence with meeting rate < 1% after 100 contacts. Test a new angle.

## Sequence Architecture Rules

### The 5-Touch Rule

Most meetings are booked on touches 3–5, not 1–2. Do not give up after 2 no-responses.

### Spacing Guidelines

| Touch | Timing | Channel |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Touch 1 | Day 1 | Email |
| Touch 2 | Day 3 | LinkedIn connection request (no message) |
| Touch 3 | Day 5 | Email (different angle) |
| Touch 4 | Day 8 | LinkedIn message (after connection) |
| Touch 5 | Day 12 | Email (final attempt — "permission to close your file?") |
| — | Day 15+ | Move to nurture (monthly touchpoint for 6 months) |

### Personalization Requirements

The first line of every email must be personalized. Generic openers (e.g., "I hope this email finds you well") are deleted without reading.

**Personalization sources:**
- Company news: recent funding, product launch, hiring surge, executive change
- Prospect's recent LinkedIn post or article
- Shared connection
- Industry-specific trigger: regulatory change, market event
- Job posting that signals the pain you solve

**Personalization formula:**
> "I noticed [specific observation]. [1-sentence connection to why that's relevant.] [Pivot to value]."

## Sequence 1: ICP Cold Email — Problem-Led

**Best for:** High-ICP prospects where you know they have the pain. Best open/reply rates.

---

**Touch 1 — Day 1 (Email)**

**Subject line options (A/B test these):**
- `[First name], quick question about [specific process]`
- `[specific pain] at [Company name]`
- `[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out`

**Body:**

```
[Personalized first line based on specific research]

[One sentence about the problem you solve — framed as a question, not a pitch.]

At [Your Company], we help [ICP job title] at [ICP company type] [specific outcome] in [timeframe]. [Logo or customer name] went from [before state] to [after state] in [X weeks].

Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if it's relevant?

[Your name]
[Title] | [Company]
[Phone — optional]
```

**Example:**

```
Saw you just raised your Series A — congrats. Scaling ops post-funding usually surfaces a specific problem: your weekly reporting takes longer each week as headcount grows.

At Reportly, we help Heads of Operations at B2B SaaS companies cut weekly report-building time from 8+ hours to under 30 minutes. Loom (after their Series A) went from 12 hours/week to 25 minutes/week in 6 weeks.

Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if it's relevant?

Alex
Head of Sales | Reportly
```

---

**Touch 2 — Day 3 (LinkedIn)**

Action: Send connection request WITH no message. A message too early is pushy. Just connect.

---

**Touch 3 — Day 5 (Email)**

**Subject line:** `Re: [copy previous subject line]` OR `Different angle, [First name]`

**Body:**

```
Following up on my note from [day of week].

I realized I led with our solution before explaining why it matters to you specifically.

[2–3 sentences describing the specific pain your ICP has, in the language of someone who has lived it — not product marketing language. Show empathy for the problem.]

We've solved this for [2–3 company names or types]. Happy to share how if you're open to a quick call.

[Your name]
```

---

**Touch 4 — Day 8 (LinkedIn message, after connection)**

**Message:**

```
Hi [First name] — I sent a couple of emails about [short description of value prop]. I don't want to keep emailing if it's not relevant, so I figured I'd ask directly here: is [specific pain] something your team is dealing with right now?

Either way, happy to connect.
```

---

**Touch 5 — Day 12 (Email — Breakup/Permission)**

**Subject line:** `Permission to close your file, [First name]?`

**Body:**

```
[First name],

I've tried reaching you a couple of times about [specific problem] — haven't heard back, so I'm guessing one of these is true:

1. Not a priority right now — totally fair.
2. Already solved — if so, I'd love to know how!
3. Wrong timing — if this is relevant in 3–6 months, happy to reconnect then.

Should I close out your file, or is there a better time to connect?

[Your name]
```

*Why this works: It gives them an easy out, which paradoxically generates more replies than another pitch.*

## Sequence 2: LinkedIn Outbound — Relationship-First

**Best for:** High-value, hard-to-reach buyers where email deliverability is difficult. Works well for enterprise or executive outreach.

---

**Touch 1 — Day 1**

Send connection request. NO message. Message too early = spam.

---

**Touch 2 — Day 3 (after acceptance)**

```
Hi [First name], thanks for connecting. I've been following [company]'s growth — [one specific observation about their company, product, or content].

I work with [ICP type] companies on [specific problem]. Curious — is [problem] something on your radar this year?

No pitch — genuinely curious about your perspective.
```

---

**Touch 3 — Day 7**

```
[First name], I wanted to share something you might find useful regardless of whether we ever work together: [link to genuinely useful article, data, or framework relevant to their role].

No agenda — just thought it was relevant given what you do.
```

---

**Touch 4 — Day 12**

```
[First name], circling back. We're working with a few companies like [company type similar to theirs] on [specific problem]. Happy to share what we're learning if it's useful — would a 20-minute call make sense?
```

---

**Touch 5 — Day 20**

```
[First name] — last message from me. I don't want to clutter your inbox.

If [specific problem] ever becomes a priority, I'd love to reconnect. Otherwise, no hard feelings — I'll leave you to it.

Either way, it's been good to connect.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 3: Post-Event / Conference Follow-Up

**Best for:** Prospects you met at a conference or event. The highest-conversion sequence when used within 24 hours.

---

**Touch 1 — Within 24 hours of event (Email or LinkedIn)**

**Subject:** `Great to meet you at [Event name]`

```
[First name],

Really enjoyed our conversation at [event] about [specific topic you discussed — BE SPECIFIC].

You mentioned [something they said] — I think there's a connection to what we do at [your company] that might be worth exploring.

Would a 20-minute call this week work to continue the conversation?

[Your name]
```

---

**Touch 2 — 3 days later (if no response)**

```
[First name], following up on my note from [day]. I know post-conference inboxes are brutal.

To make this easy: [Calendly or booking link] — grab a slot that works for you, or ignore this if the timing isn't right.

[Your name]
```

---

**Touch 3 — 10 days later (if still no response)**

```
[First name], last follow-up from me — I know it was a busy week.

I mentioned [specific value prop relevant to their problem]. If that ever becomes relevant, I'm at [email]. Otherwise, I'll stop cluttering your inbox.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 4: Re-Engagement — Previous Prospect (Gone Dark)

**Best for:** Deals that went silent after a meeting or evaluation. Cadence: different from fresh outreach — fewer, more spaced touches.

---

**Touch 1 — Day 1 (Email)**

**Subject:** `Checking in, [First name]`

```
[First name],

It's been [X weeks] since we last spoke. I didn't want to assume our conversation had run its course without checking in.

A lot has changed since we talked: [1–2 sentences of genuine news — product update, customer win, relevant market development].

Is this still on your radar, or has something changed on your end?

[Your name]
```

---

**Touch 2 — Day 7**

```
[First name], not sure if my last note landed in spam.

I'll be direct: when we spoke in [month], you mentioned [specific pain they described]. Has that been resolved, or is it still an issue?

Happy to jump on a quick call to pick up where we left off.

[Your name]
```

---

**Touch 3 — Day 21 (Final re-engagement)**

**Subject:** `Should I close your file?`

```
[First name],

I've reached out a few times without hearing back. I want to respect your time, so I'll ask plainly: is it better for me to close out your file and check back in [X months], or is there a conversation still worth having?

No judgment either way — I just want to be useful, not annoying.

[Your name]
```

## Subject Line Swipe File

Tested subject lines by category:

### Curiosity / Direct Question
- `Quick question about [specific thing]`
- `How does [company] handle [problem]?`
- `[First name] — is [pain] costing you?`

### Social Proof
- `How [similar company] solved [problem]`
- `[Customer name] said I should reach out`
- `[Metric: "cut report time by 80%"] — how we did it`

### Personalization Hook
- `Your post on [topic] made me think...`
- `[Company] just raised — question about what comes next`
- `Noticed [company] is hiring [role] — might be related`

### Re-engagement
- `Permission to close your file?`
- `Should I stop reaching out?`
- `Last message from me, [First name]`

### Avoid (low open rates)
- "Following up on my previous email"
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Just checking in"
- One-word subjects ("Hi", "Hello")
- ALL CAPS subjects

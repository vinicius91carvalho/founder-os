# Product Hunt Launch Checklist

> A complete checklist for planning and executing a Product Hunt launch. Product Hunt works best for B2B SaaS, developer tools, productivity tools, and consumer apps. Work through each section in sequence — do not skip steps.

---

## Is Product Hunt Right for Your Launch?

Product Hunt is most effective when your target customer is: developers, designers, product managers, tech-savvy founders, or early adopters of software tools.

**Good fit for PH:**
- SaaS tools for technical or business professionals
- Developer tools, APIs, open-source projects
- Productivity and workflow apps
- AI tools and apps

**Poor fit for PH:**
- B2B enterprise software (buyers don't browse PH)
- Local services businesses
- Traditional consumer goods
- Regulated industries (finance, healthcare) without tech audience

**Self-assessment:**
- [ ] My target customer visits Product Hunt at least occasionally
- [ ] My product solves a specific, describable problem for a PH audience
- [ ] I have a working product (not just a landing page — PH users want to try it)

## 30 Days Before Launch

### Find a Hunter

A "hunter" is someone who submits your product on Product Hunt. Being submitted by an influencer with PH following boosts initial visibility.

- [ ] **Identify potential hunters:** Look for PH users with > 1,000 followers who have hunted similar products. Tools: ph.com/leaderboard, @producthunt_ship, hunter-specific databases
- [ ] **Reach out to 5–10 potential hunters:** Personal, specific message — not a form email. Explain what your product does and why their audience would care
- [ ] **Secure a hunter OR plan to self-hunt:** If no influencer hunter responds, self-hunting is acceptable and becoming increasingly common (PH reduced the hunting boost in 2021)

**Hunter outreach message:**
```
Hi [Name],

I've followed your Product Hunt activity — you've hunted several tools in [space].

I'm launching [Product], which [one-line description targeting your audience]. Our beta
users describe it as [customer quote]. We launch on [date].

Would you be interested in hunting it? I'll have all the assets ready 48 hours in advance.

[Your name]
```

---

### Build Your Maker Account

- [ ] **Create or verify your PH maker account** at producthunt.com
- [ ] **Complete your profile:** Real photo, bio, company link, Twitter/X link. A complete profile builds trust with the community
- [ ] **Engage with the community for 2–3 weeks before launch:** Comment on other products, upvote things you genuinely like. Build at least 50+ activity points before launch — it signals you're a real community member, not just here to promote
- [ ] **Follow 50+ relevant Product Hunt users** — they'll see your notification when you launch

### Prepare All Launch Assets

- [ ] **Product thumbnail (240×240px):** Square, no text, distinctive visual identity. This is the first thing people see. Spend time on it.
  - [ ] Tested on both light and dark background
  - [ ] Brand colors consistent with product
  - [ ] Memorable at small size (thumbnail grid view)

- [ ] **Tagline (≤ 60 characters):** The one-line description. Should clearly explain what the product does and who it's for. NO jargon.
  - Draft 1: [___________]
  - Draft 2: [___________]
  - Draft 3: [___________]
  - Final: [___________]

- [ ] **Gallery images (4–6 screenshots):**
  - [ ] Image 1: Hero image — the primary value moment (what the product looks like doing the thing it does best)
  - [ ] Image 2: Key feature 1
  - [ ] Image 3: Key feature 2
  - [ ] Image 4: Social proof (customer quote or metric)
  - [ ] Image 5: Before/after comparison (optional but powerful)
  - [ ] All images are 1270×952px (recommended PH gallery size)
  - [ ] All images have captions

- [ ] **Demo video (optional but strongly recommended — 60 seconds):**
  - [ ] Opens with the problem (10 seconds)
  - [ ] Shows product solving the problem (40 seconds)
  - [ ] Clear CTA at the end (10 seconds)
  - [ ] No talking head required — screen recording with voiceover works fine
  - [ ] Captions for accessibility

- [ ] **Description text (PH allows Markdown):**
  - [ ] Opening paragraph: One paragraph on the problem and who it affects
  - [ ] Product description: What it does and how it works
  - [ ] Key features: Bullet list of 3–5 features (not a dump of every feature)
  - [ ] Social proof: 1–2 customer quotes
  - [ ] Call to action: "Try it free at [URL]"

---

### Prepare the Maker's First Comment

The Maker's First Comment is pinned at the top of all comments. It's your most valuable real estate. Write it before launch day.

- [ ] **Draft the Maker's First Comment (200–400 words):**

  Structure:
  1. Personal story — why you built this (2–3 sentences). Be authentic.
  2. The specific problem — describe it vividly, in the customer's language (2–3 sentences)
  3. What makes your approach different — the key insight (2–3 sentences)
  4. What you're asking for — "Harsh feedback welcome — we're just [X] weeks in"
  5. Offer — "DM me for extended trial" or "Happy to chat 1:1 with anyone in [target sector]"

  Draft:
  ```
  [Write your first comment here — aim for 250–350 words]
  ```

## 7 Days Before Launch

- [ ] **Confirm launch date and time:** 12:01 AM Pacific Time for maximum exposure window (full 24 hours). Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday for best traffic.
- [ ] **Submit product to PH 24–48 hours in advance** (if hunter can schedule it). This allows pre-launch review.
- [ ] **Notify your launch team:** Who is monitoring PH on launch day? Assign roles:
  - PH comment monitor and responder: [Name]
  - Twitter/X social amplification: [Name]
  - Email outreach to supporters: [Name]
  - Customer support: [Name]
- [ ] **Brief your early supporters:**
  - [ ] Drafted personal email to 50–100 people with: exact time, exact link, what kind of engagement helps (honest comments, not just upvotes)
  - [ ] Do NOT ask people to upvote — PH penalizes gaming. Ask them to "check it out and share if it's relevant to them"

**Support email template:**
```
Subject: [Product] launches on Product Hunt on [date] — wanted to let you know

Hi [Name],

Quick heads up — we're launching [Product] on Product Hunt on [DATE] at 12:01 AM PT.

If you've used the product and found it valuable, a comment sharing your experience
(honest — including any feedback) would mean a lot. We want real signal, not fluff.

Link: [PH URL — available after submission]

Thanks for being part of the early community.

[Your name]
```

## Launch Day Execution

**Target: Every comment gets a response within 60 minutes on launch day.**

### Minute 0 (12:01 AM PT)

- [ ] Confirm product is live on PH
- [ ] Publish the Maker's First Comment immediately
- [ ] Send notification email to your early supporter list
- [ ] Post on Twitter/X: "We just launched on @ProductHunt — [tagline]. Would love your feedback: [PH link]"
- [ ] Post on LinkedIn (if applicable)
- [ ] Post in relevant communities where you have credibility (Slack groups, Discord servers, subreddits)

### Hours 1–6

- [ ] Respond to every PH comment personally. Even "looks cool" deserves a thank you and one line about something specific
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions in comments to generate more engagement ("Great question — for your use case specifically, here's how we handle [X]")
- [ ] Respond to every Twitter/LinkedIn mention
- [ ] Monitor for product issues — first 100 users testing your product simultaneously will find bugs

### Hours 6–12

- [ ] Post a "maker update" as a comment in your PH thread: "5 hours in — we've had [X] signups and the most common feedback is [Y]. We're already working on [Z]." Real-time transparency is unusual and well-received.
- [ ] If you're in the top 10: amplify further — the ranking algorithm rewards continued engagement
- [ ] Reach out directly to 5–10 users who left especially detailed comments — offer a personal call

### Hours 12–24

- [ ] Final push on social if you're close to a top position
- [ ] Post "end of day" update in PH comments
- [ ] Thank your hunter (if applicable)
- [ ] Document all feedback for product roadmap

## Post-Launch (48 Hours — 7 Days After)

- [ ] **Send follow-up email to all new signups** (within 24 hours): Personal welcome from the founder, specific next step to get value from the product
- [ ] **Respond to any outstanding PH comments** (some people comment days later)
- [ ] **Collect and publish metrics:** "Here's what happened on our Product Hunt launch day" — published as a blog post or Twitter thread. This extends the lifecycle of the launch
- [ ] **Identify top 3 new customers from PH** — reach out personally, offer a 1:1 call, use for case study
- [ ] **Submit to PH newsletters:** If you performed well (top 5 of the day), email team@producthunt.com with a brief summary — you may be featured in the weekly digest

## Success Metrics and Benchmarks

| Metric | Typical Range | Good | Great |
|---|---|---|---|
| Upvotes (Day 1) | 100–500 | 500–1,000 | 1,000+ |
| PH comments | 20–50 | 50–100 | 100+ |
| Signups from PH | 50–200 | 200–500 | 500+ |
| Paying customers from PH | 2–10 | 10–30 | 30+ |
| Product of the Day ranking | Top 10 | Top 5 | #1 |

**Important calibration:** "Product of the Day #1" does not guarantee product success or revenue. Many PH #1 products have failed; many successful products placed in the top 5–10 on their launch day. Engagement quality (real customers with genuine problems) matters more than upvote count.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it hurts | What to do instead |
|---|---|---|
| Launching without a working product | PH community upvotes then forgets — no retention | Wait until core value is demonstrable |
| No Maker's First Comment | First impressions are set by the product description alone — no authentic human signal | Write and publish immediately at 12:01 AM |
| Asking for upvotes | PH detects and penalizes vote manipulation — can get listing removed | Ask for honest engagement only |
| One-word responses to comments | Low engagement signals = algorithmic suppression | Write substantive, specific responses |
| No email capture on landing page | PH traffic is a spike — you need to capture it | Live email capture that works before launch |
| Wrong day (weekend) | Saturday traffic is 40–60% of weekday traffic | Launch Tuesday–Thursday |
| No follow-up sequence | Signups forget they signed up | 3-email onboarding sequence ready before launch |

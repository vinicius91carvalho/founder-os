## B2B Tactics Matrix

### Tactic 1: Founder-Led Outbound (LinkedIn + Email)

**Best for:** B2B SaaS, professional services, tools targeting specific job roles
**Timeline to first customers:** 1–3 weeks
**CAC:** $0–$500 (time-based)
**Effort:** High
**Scalability:** Low (until handed off to sales hire)

**Execution steps:**

**Step 1 — Define your outreach ICP (this is the outreach-level version of your customer archetype):**
```
Job title: [Specific title(s)]
Company size: [employee range or revenue range]
Industry: [Specific sector(s)]
Trigger signal (what makes them need your product NOW):
[e.g., "recently funded", "just hired a 5th ops person", "posted about this problem on LinkedIn"]
Location (if relevant): [Geography]
LinkedIn filter: [How to find them on LinkedIn — which filters]
```

**Step 2 — Build the prospect list:**
- Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator (free trial sufficient for 50–100 prospects) or LinkedIn search
- Find 100 prospects who match the outreach ICP
- Identify if any have posted about or engaged with content related to your problem
- Export to Google Sheets / CRM

**Step 3 — Write the outreach sequence (3 touches):**

Touch 1 — LinkedIn connection request message (< 300 characters):
```
Hi [Name], I noticed [specific thing about them — their post, their company, their role change].
I'm working on [one-line description of product] and think it's relevant to [their specific situation].
Would love to connect.
```

Touch 2 — LinkedIn DM or email after connection (2–3 days after connecting):
```
Subject: [Specific problem they have — not your product name]

Hi [Name],

[One specific observation about their situation — from their LinkedIn, company, or industry context]

I'm building [company] which [solves specific problem]. We're working with [2–3 companies
like theirs] and I think we could help [specific outcome for them].

Would a 20-minute call to explore if it's relevant be worth your time?

[Your name]
```

Touch 3 — Follow-up (5 business days later, if no response):
```
Hi [Name],

Just following up on my last message. Happy to share a quick demo or case study
if that's more useful than a call.

Would 20 minutes this week work?

[Your name]
```

**Metrics to track:**
- Connection acceptance rate (target: > 30%)
- Response rate to Touch 2 (target: > 10%)
- Meeting booking rate (target: > 5% of prospected)
- Meeting to paid conversion rate (target: > 20%)

**Weekly target:** 25 new prospects contacted → 2–3 meetings → 0–1 new customer per week

### Tactic 2: Community-Led Outreach

**Best for:** Developer tools, productivity tools, niche B2B products, any product whose customers gather in online communities
**Timeline to first customers:** 2–6 weeks
**CAC:** $0–$100 (time-based)
**Effort:** Medium
**Scalability:** Medium

**Community inventory — find where your customers gather:**

| Community | Platform | Member count | Activity level | Relevance (1–5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Community 1] | Reddit / Slack / Discord / FB | | | |
| [Community 2] | | | | |
| [Community 3] | | | | |
| [Community 4] | | | | |
| [Community 5] | | | | |

**Prioritize:** Communities with > 1,000 members, active daily posting, and direct relevance to your customer's problem

**Execution cadence:**

Week 1–2: Lurk and observe. Don't post. Read 50+ threads to understand the language, the recurring questions, and the community norms.

Week 2: Post ONE helpful piece of content — a framework, a resource list, an analysis of the problem your product solves. No product mention. Aim for useful.

Week 3: Respond substantively to 5–10 threads per week where your expertise adds value. No product mention. Build credibility first.

Week 4+: When you have 10+ helpful contributions, introduce your product in context. "I kept seeing this question come up so I built a tool that solves it — [link]. Happy to give anyone here free access."

**Anti-pattern:** Joining a community and immediately promoting your product. This is the #1 banned behavior in most communities and will get you removed.

### Tactic 3: Partner / Integration Channel

**Best for:** Tools that integrate with other tools (Salesforce, Notion, Slack, QuickBooks integrations)
**Timeline to first customers:** 4–12 weeks (partner conversations are slow)
**CAC:** Revenue share or time-based (varies)
**Effort:** Medium
**Scalability:** High if partnership works

**Execution steps:**

1. **Identify integration partners:** Which tools do your customers already use? Build integrations with the top 2–3.

2. **Get listed in partner marketplaces:** Salesforce AppExchange, HubSpot App Marketplace, Notion Integrations, Zapier, Make.com. Listings are free and produce long-tail inbound.

3. **Co-marketing with complementary tools:** Identify tools that serve the same customer but don't compete. Propose: blog post exchange, newsletter mention, product webinar together.

**Target metrics:** 1–2 partnerships activated in first 90 days. Each partnership should produce > 5 customers to be worth continued investment.

## B2C Tactics Matrix

### Tactic 4: Reddit-First Community Strategy

**Best for:** Consumer products addressing a pain that communities discuss openly
**Timeline to first customers:** 2–6 weeks
**CAC:** $0–$50 (time-based)
**Effort:** Medium-High
**Scalability:** Medium

**Find your subreddits:**

Target subreddits: communities where your customer discusses the problem your product solves — NOT startup subreddits, NOT r/entrepreneur.

**Content approach:**

Type 1 — Problem-first post: "I spent 6 months solving [specific problem]. Here's what I learned." Share genuine insights. Mention product in comments only when asked.

Type 2 — Resource post: "The best tools for [specific problem] — a ranked list." Include your product in the list alongside 5–10 others. Honesty builds trust.

Type 3 — Story post: "I built [product] after [personal experience with problem]. Here's the story." Authentic founder stories resonate in communities.

**Metrics:** Track referral traffic from Reddit → signups → paid. If traffic is high but conversion is low, landing page copy isn't matching what the Reddit audience expected.

### Tactic 5: Influencer Seeding (Micro-Influencer First)

**Best for:** Consumer products with visual appeal or demonstrable value
**Timeline to first customers:** 2–4 weeks
**CAC:** $0–$500 per influencer activation
**Effort:** Medium
**Scalability:** Medium

**Influencer identification:**

Don't target macro-influencers (500K+ followers). Target micro-influencers (1K–50K followers) in your exact niche. They have:
- Higher engagement rates (5–15% vs. 0.5–2% for macro)
- More trusting audience relationships
- More affordable or free access (product-for-review)
- More willingness to genuinely engage with new products

**Outreach for micro-influencers:**

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following your content on [topic] — specifically [specific post that shows you've
actually watched their content].

I built [product] to solve [the specific problem they cover], and I think their audience would
genuinely find it useful.

I'd love to offer you free lifetime access in exchange for an honest review — positive or not.
No performance requirement, no script.

Would that be interesting?

[Your name]
```

**Product-for-review terms:**
- Free lifetime access or extended trial
- No paid placement (honest review only)
- Right to share the review on your site (if positive)

**Target:** 10 micro-influencer activations → expect 2–3 posts/reviews → 20–100 signups per review for niche audiences

### Tactic 6: SEO and Content First

**Best for:** Products addressing a problem people actively search for (high search intent)
**Timeline to first customers:** 8–16 weeks (SEO is slow)
**CAC:** $50–$300 (content creation cost)
**Effort:** Medium
**Scalability:** Very high (compounds over time)

**Only use this as a primary channel if:**
- Your target keyword (the problem people search for) has ≥ 1,000 monthly searches
- You can write genuine, expert-level content (not AI-generated template content — Google penalizes thin content)
- You have 3–6 months of patience before significant traffic arrives

**Content strategy for first 100 customers:**

1. **Identify 10 target keywords** — problems your customer searches for, not product names:
   - "How to [solve specific problem]"
   - "[Problem] tools comparison"
   - "[Problem] template free"
   - "Best [tool category] for [specific customer type]"

2. **Write 10 blog posts** — one per keyword. Each post should be the best resource on the internet for that query. Minimum 1,500 words. Use real data, real examples, real screenshots.

3. **Build backlinks** — Guest posts on industry blogs, resource page submissions, and community link sharing. 5–10 backlinks per article accelerates ranking.

4. **Conversion optimization** — Each article needs a clear CTA to the product. Best CTA types: Free tool related to the article topic, downloadable template, product demo video.

## Referral Mechanics (Works for Both B2C and B2B)

**Referral is only worth building after 20+ paying customers. Before that, you don't have enough base to generate referral volume.**

**Basic referral program structure:**

| Mechanic | How it works | Good for |
|---|---|---|
| Double-sided discount | Referrer gets X% off next month, referred gets X% off first month | SaaS with monthly pricing |
| Cash credit | Referrer gets $X account credit per referral | High-ticket SaaS or marketplace |
| Free month | Referrer gets free month for each paying referral | Subscription products |
| Tier upgrade | Referrer gets upgraded to next tier for 3 months per referral | Tiered SaaS products |

**The referral ask (when to ask):**

Ask for referrals at the "success moment" — the specific point in onboarding where a customer first realizes the product is working for them. Not at signup. Not at 30 days. At the moment they say "oh, this actually works."

For your product, the success moment is:
> [Define the specific event that indicates a customer has gotten value — e.g., "first invoice auto-reconciled," "first meeting booked," "first report generated"]

Automated trigger: When a customer reaches the success moment event, send:
> "Glad you hit [milestone]! If you know anyone else at [company type] who deals with [problem], a personal introduction from you means more than any ad we could run."

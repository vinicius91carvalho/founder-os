# Board Deck Template

> **Purpose:** Structure your quarterly board meeting to be productive, not performative. The goal is a high-quality decision-making conversation, not a status presentation.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This template was generated by an AI system. Board governance varies by company stage, investor rights, and jurisdiction. Financial projections and business metrics discussed herein should be reviewed with your legal and financial advisors. Include a Financial Disclaimer if projections are presented to investors.

---

## Before You Build the Deck

**Send the deck 5 days before the meeting.** Board members should arrive having read it. The meeting is for discussion, not for the board to read your slides for the first time.

**Pre-meeting call with your lead investor:** Share any major news (good or bad) before the board meeting. Surprises destroy trust. A 15-minute call the week before prevents emotional reactions in the meeting.

**Time budget for a 2-hour board meeting:**
- CEO narrative update: 10 minutes
- Financials (questions on pre-read): 15 minutes
- Key strategic topic #1: 30 minutes
- Key strategic topic #2: 20 minutes
- Asks and next steps: 15 minutes
- Buffer / overrun: 30 minutes

## Slide 6: Asks

*This is the highest-leverage part of the meeting. Boards can open doors, make introductions, share expertise, and offer advice. But only if you ask specifically.*

**Rule:** Vague asks get no action. Specific asks get done.

| # | Ask | Context | Who |
|---|-----|---------|-----|
| 1 | Intro to [specific person] at [specific company] | [Why you want this intro — what it would enable] | [Which board member has this connection] |
| 2 | Advice on [specific decision] | [What decision you're facing, what you've considered, what you need] | [Open to all / specific board member] |
| 3 | Connection to [specific type of person] | [What you're looking for and why] | [Open to all] |

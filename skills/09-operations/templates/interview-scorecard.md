# Interview Scorecard Template

> **Purpose:** Standardize interview evaluation to reduce bias, improve consistency across interviewers, and make hiring decisions based on evidence rather than impression. Structured interviews with independent scoring have 2–3x better predictive validity than unstructured interviews.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This template was generated by an AI system. Structured interviews reduce but do not eliminate bias. Review your process with an HR professional to ensure compliance with employment law in your jurisdiction. This template is a framework — adapt it to your role and legal context.

---

## How to Use This Scorecard

**Before the interview:**
1. Fill in the role title, competencies, and questions before your first interview
2. Each interviewer gets their OWN copy — do not share scores during the process
3. Agree on the rating scale definition in a calibration meeting before interviewing

**During the interview:**
- Take notes on specific quotes and behaviors — you'll forget details within hours
- Ask follow-up probes: "Tell me more about that" / "What would you do differently?" / "What was your specific contribution?"
- Don't signal your reaction — maintain neutral body language regardless of the answer

**After the interview:**
- Complete your scorecard within 1 hour of the interview (memory degrades fast)
- Submit your score before the debrief — do NOT discuss with other interviewers first
- In the debrief, share scores first, then discuss reasons

## Debrief Protocol

*Run this process at the end of every interview loop:*

1. **Collect scorecards** — All interviewers submit before the debrief starts
2. **Share scores anonymously** (or simultaneously) — Say "On 3, everyone share your overall score: 1, 2, 3..."
3. **Share evidence, not impressions** — Each person cites specific moments. "I scored them a 2 on ownership because they said [quote] which signals..."
4. **Identify divergence** — If two interviewers are 2+ points apart on the same competency, probe the disagreement. Different interpretations of the rubric or different evidence?
5. **Check for anchoring bias** — If the most senior person in the room shares their opinion first, others anchor to it. Consider having the most junior person share first.
6. **Make a decision** — Don't leave without a clear yes/no. "We need more info" requires a specific follow-up action with a timeline, not another indefinite hold.

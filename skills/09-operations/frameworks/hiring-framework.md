# Hiring Framework: End-to-End Process

> **Purpose:** A repeatable process from identifying a hiring need through day-30 of the new hire. Every step exists because skipping it has a documented failure mode.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This framework was generated by an AI system. Employment law varies significantly by jurisdiction. Before implementing any hiring process, consult with an employment attorney or HR professional in your area. This framework is a guide, not legal advice.

---

## Phase 0: Is This Hire Ready?

Before writing a JD or opening a requisition, answer four questions:

1. **Outcome clarity** — Can you describe exactly what this person will have produced in 90 days? If you can't, the role isn't defined. Don't start the process.
2. **Budget approval** — Is salary, benefits, and total comp budgeted for the full year? (Not "we'll figure it out" — actual approved numbers.)
3. **Manager readiness** — Does the hiring manager have capacity to onboard and develop this person? A bad onboarding experience costs you the hire.
4. **Process readiness** — Do you have a scorecard built for this role? If not, build it before the first interview.

**Gate:** All four must be YES before Phase 1.

## Phase 1: Role Definition (Week 1)

### Step 1.1 — Write the Outcome Profile (not the JD)

The Outcome Profile precedes the JD. It's internal — for your own clarity:

```
Role: [Title]
Department: [Function]
Reports to: [Title]

Why this role exists NOW:
[1 paragraph: What specific problem does this person solve? What breaks without this hire?]

Outcomes at 30 days:
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]

Outcomes at 90 days:
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]

Outcomes at 6 months:
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [What "great" looks like]

Non-outcomes (what this role does NOT own):
- [Boundary 1]
- [Boundary 2]
```

### Step 1.2 — Define 4–5 Competencies

From the Outcome Profile, identify the 4–5 capabilities that make someone able to produce those outcomes. Use this checklist to generate candidates:

- **Problem-solving / analytical rigor** — ability to break down complex problems
- **Customer empathy** — understanding of user needs and pain points
- **Communication clarity** — written and verbal communication at their level
- **Ownership and accountability** — drives outcomes without being managed
- **Cross-functional influence** — gets things done without authority
- **Technical depth** — [domain-specific technical skill]
- **Growth mindset** — learns from failure, adapts quickly
- **Leadership and team development** — builds and grows a team (for manager roles)

Choose 4–5 most critical for THIS role. These become your scorecard dimensions.

### Step 1.3 — Write the Job Description

Use `templates/job-description.md`. Publish internally (to team, for referrals) before posting publicly.

## Phase 2: Sourcing (Weeks 1–3)

### Sourcing Channel Strategy

**Target mix (early-stage):**
- 30% — Employee referrals (highest conversion, fastest time-to-hire)
- 30% — Outbound sourcing (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, community networks)
- 40% — Inbound applicants (job boards, company careers page)

**Referral activation:**
- Share the Outcome Profile with your team (not just the JD)
- Ask specifically: "Who do you know who is great at [specific competency]?"
- Set a referral bonus policy before asking ($2–5k for non-exec roles is standard at early stage)
- Follow up on every referral within 24 hours — your team is watching

**Outbound sourcing:**
- Build a "target list" of 10 specific people who fit the role before posting the JD
- Source from: competitor alumni, community members, conference speakers, podcast guests, open-source contributors
- Personalize every outreach message — reference their specific work

**Inbound channels by role type:**
| Role | Best Boards |
|------|------------|
| Engineering | LinkedIn, Wellfound (AngelList), specific GitHub communities |
| Design | Dribbble, Behance, LinkedIn, Cofolios |
| Product | Lenny's Newsletter jobs, ProductHunt, LinkedIn |
| Sales / Marketing | LinkedIn, specific industry newsletters |
| Operations / Finance | LinkedIn, specific professional associations |
| All | Your company's social channels, email newsletter |

### Sourcing SLA

- Post JD: Day 1 of Phase 2
- First outbound messages: Day 1–3
- Follow-up on referrals: Within 24 hours of referral
- First pipeline review: End of Week 2
- Close sourcing (unless pipeline empty): End of Week 3

## Phase 3: Screening (Weeks 2–4)

### Application Review

- Review all applications within 48 hours of receipt
- Use a simple screening rubric (not subjective): Does their stated experience indicate they've produced outcomes like the ones in the profile? Yes / Maybe / No
- Do not use education, age, or other proxies. Evaluate evidence of outcomes only.

### Recruiter / Founder Screen (30 minutes, video)

**Purpose:** Validate mutual interest and surface disqualifying mismatches before investing both parties' time.

**Questions to cover:**
1. Walk me through your last 2–3 years — what did you own, what did you produce?
2. What specifically drew you to this role?
3. What are your compensation expectations? [Get this on the table early — don't invest 4 rounds only to discover misalignment]
4. What are your timing and availability?
5. What questions do you have about the role or company?

**Pass criteria:**
- Evidence of producing relevant outcomes (not just doing the tasks)
- Genuine fit with what the role requires (not a stretch hire hoping they'll grow into it)
- Compensation expectations within range (confirm; don't guarantee)
- Timeline compatible with your needs

**Disposition:** Screen pass → advance to Interview Round 1. Screen fail → decline within 24 hours with brief, respectful note.

## Phase 4: Interview Process (Weeks 3–5)

### Interview Round Structure

| Round | Format | Duration | Competencies Evaluated | Who |
|-------|--------|----------|----------------------|-----|
| Round 1: Behavioral | Video/phone | 60 min | 2–3 core competencies | Hiring manager |
| Round 2: Skills | Video/in-person | 90 min | 1–2 technical/domain competencies + 1 work sample or case | Peer / domain expert |
| Round 3: Executive / Culture | Video/in-person | 45–60 min | Values alignment, long-term fit, leadership | Founder / senior leader |
| References | Calls | 20 min each | Validation of scorecard scores | Hiring manager |

*For early-stage companies: compress to 2 rounds + references if timeline is critical. Never compress by removing the scorecard — only by merging rounds.*

### Scorecard Process

1. All interviewers use `templates/interview-scorecard.md` — same competencies, different rounds
2. Scores submitted before the debrief — no exceptions
3. Debrief runs within 48 hours of final interview
4. Hiring recommendation must correlate with scorecard scores — if you're overriding the scorecard, document why explicitly

### Work Sample / Case Study Guidelines

- Relevant to actual work they'd do in the role (not trick questions)
- Maximum 3 hours of effort — respect their time
- Provide clear instructions and evaluation criteria in advance
- Evaluate in a structured way (not "I liked it" / "I didn't like it")
- Consider paying for substantial work samples (builds goodwill; signals you value their time)

### Reference Check Protocol

Do references after final round, before offer. References are not a formality — they are the highest-signal interview.

**Who to call:**
- At least 1 direct manager
- At least 1 peer or collaborator
- Optional: 1 direct report (if management role)

**Questions:**
1. In what context did you work with [Name]?
2. What were they specifically responsible for producing?
3. What did they do better than almost anyone else at their level?
4. What was their biggest growth area? What would they tell you themselves?
5. How did they handle [specific challenge relevant to this role]?
6. Would you hire them again? If yes: immediately or with conditions? If no: what would need to be different?
7. Is there anything I should ask them directly that I haven't already?

**Red flags in references:**
- Reference refuses to comment beyond "yes they worked here" (can indicate legal issue)
- Unprompted hedging on a core competency
- Inability to provide a specific example of the candidate producing outcomes
- "Great person but..." (the information is after the "but")

## Phase 5: Decision and Offer (Week 5–6)

### Decision Criteria

| Score | Interpretation | Decision |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| 18–20 / 20 | Exceptional | Offer immediately |
| 14–17 / 20 | Strong hire | Offer with confidence |
| 10–13 / 20 | Acceptable | Offer only if: (1) role is hard to fill, AND (2) concerns are in developable areas |
| < 10 / 20 | Below bar | Do not offer. Reopen search. |

**The bar:** Ask "Would I be thrilled to have this person join?" A "yes" is a yes. A "maybe" is a no.

### Offer Construction

**Compensation components:**
- **Base salary:** Market range for role, level, and geography. Research: Levels.fyi (tech), LinkedIn Salary, Glassdoor, Radford (if you have access)
- **Equity:** Use vesting schedule template. Communicate total grant size AND vesting schedule AND 409A price AND strike price.
- **Benefits:** Health, dental, vision, 401k match, PTO policy, parental leave — be explicit
- **Start date:** Confirm availability

**Offer process:**
1. Verbal offer (call, not email): Share the offer and gauge reaction before sending paperwork
2. Written offer letter: Within 24 hours of verbal acceptance
3. Offer deadline: Give 3–5 business days for non-executive roles. Exploding offers (< 48 hours) are disrespectful and backfire.
4. Negotiation: Have a clear policy before the call. Typical early-stage: ±5–10% base; equity is generally fixed.

## Phase 6: Pre-Boarding and Day 1 (Weeks 6–8)

### Pre-Boarding (Between offer acceptance and start date)

- Send welcome email from CEO/founder within 24 hours of signed offer
- Set up accounts, equipment, and access before Day 1
- Assign onboarding buddy (peer-level, not manager)
- Send company handbook, culture doc, first-week agenda
- Optional: Intro call with team lead 1 week before start (reduces anxiety)

### First 30 Days

| Week | Focus | Outcome |
|------|-------|---------|
| Week 1 | Context | Understands company mission, product, customers, team |
| Week 2 | Role | Understands their specific outcomes, tools, processes |
| Week 3 | Contribution | First small deliverable — builds confidence and demonstrates ownership |
| Week 4 | Calibration | 30-day check-in with manager: "What's unclear? What's harder than expected? What needs to change?" |

**30-day check-in questions (manager to new hire):**
1. What have you learned that surprised you?
2. What's unclear about your role or expectations?
3. What do you need from me to be successful?
4. Is there anything you expected to be different about this company/role?
5. On a 1–10 scale, how confident are you that you can hit your 90-day outcomes? What would move that number up?

## Process Metrics

Track these to improve your hiring process over time:

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Time-to-fill | ≤ 45 days (non-exec) / ≤ 90 days (exec) | Longer = broken sourcing or decision process |
| Offer acceptance rate | ≥ 85% | Low = compensation misalignment or bad experience |
| 90-day retention | ≥ 90% | Low = screening or onboarding failure |
| 12-month retention | ≥ 80% | Low = role design or culture issue |
| Referral conversion rate | 2–4x inbound | If low = referral program isn't activated |
| Scorecard correlation with performance | Track at 6 months | If low = scorecard isn't measuring the right things |

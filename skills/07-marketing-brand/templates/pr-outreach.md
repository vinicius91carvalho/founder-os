# PR Outreach Templates

> Press release and media pitch templates for startup founders. PR is most effective when you have a real story — traction data, a significant funding round, or genuinely novel research.

---

## When to Pursue PR

| You have | PR tier | Target outlets |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| Funding round ($500K+) | A — National tech press | TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired, VentureBeat |
| Funding round ($10M+) | A — General business press | WSJ, Bloomberg, Forbes, Fortune |
| Significant traction milestone (users, revenue) | A/B | Tech press + trade press for your vertical |
| Novel research or data | A/B | Tech press, business press, trade publications |
| Vertical-specific story | B — Trade press | Publications specific to your industry |
| Local / regional story | C — Local media | Local business journals, regional newspapers |
| Founder story / personal narrative | B/C | Features desks of relevant publications |
| Product launch (no traction) | Not yet | Blog post, ProductHunt, owned channels |

**Key rule:** No journalist will cover "we launched a startup." Your story must answer: why does this matter to my readers NOW?

## Part 1: Finding and Qualifying Journalists

### Step 1: Build a Journalist Target List

Before pitching anyone, build a targeted list. Mass pitching is how you end up on spam lists.

**Qualification criteria for each journalist:**
- [ ] Has written about companies in your category (search: `site:techcrunch.com [your category]`)
- [ ] Has covered funding rounds at your stage
- [ ] Most recent coverage is within 6 months (covering your beat actively)
- [ ] Email is findable (Hunter.io, their Twitter bio, their publication's staff page)

**Target list template:**

| Journalist | Publication | Beat / Coverage area | Recent relevant article | Contact | Status |
|------------|-------------|---------------------|------------------------|---------|--------|
| [Name] | [Publication] | [e.g., "Enterprise SaaS, B2B tools"] | [Title + link] | [email@pub.com] | [ ] Not contacted / [ ] Pitched / [ ] Responded |
| [Name] | | | | | |
| [Name] | | | | | |

**Target: 5–10 qualified journalists per story. Do NOT pitch 50 people the same email.**

### Step 2: Warm the Relationship Before Pitching

The cold pitch success rate is < 5%. Warm outreach is 3–5× higher.

**Warming tactics (4–8 weeks before your pitch):**
- Follow them on Twitter/X. Engage thoughtfully with their articles (replies, not likes).
- Share their articles with genuine commentary (not just tagging them).
- If they're a prolific tweeter, engage with their non-work content naturally.
- Be a source: "Happy to connect you with our Head of [X] for perspective on [topic they cover]" — no pitch, just source availability.

## Part 2: The Media Pitch

### What Makes a Pitch Work

A good pitch answers in 3 seconds: "Why should I cover this?" and "Why now?"

**Structure:**
1. **Hook (1 sentence):** The most compelling part of your story
2. **Context (1–2 sentences):** Why this matters / what's happening in the industry
3. **Your news (2–3 sentences):** What you're announcing and the proof
4. **Data (1–2 data points):** Make it specific; numbers kill generic pitches
5. **Offer (1 sentence):** What you're offering them (exclusive, interview, data)

**Total length: Under 200 words.** Journalists get 100+ pitches per day. Long pitches are deleted.

### Email Pitch Template — Funding Announcement

**Subject line options (A/B test):**
- `[Company] raises $[X]M to [1-line value prop] — exclusive?`
- `[Founder first name] + [Company] — [#]M round, [key metric]`
- `Funding story: [Company] | [X]M | [category]`

**Body:**

```
Hi [First name],

[Hook:] [Company] just closed a $[X]M [seed/Series A/etc.] round — [why this matters in one sentence that isn't just "to grow"].

[Context:] [1 sentence about the market problem or trend this fits into — make it relevant to their readers.]

[News:] We're announcing [funding amount], led by [lead investor] with [others]. We'll use it to [specific use — "double engineering headcount" not "scale our platform"].

[Proof:] [Key metric: e.g., "We're growing 40% MoM with [# customers/ARR]."]

[Offer:] Happy to offer you an exclusive before the embargo lifts on [date]. [Optional: I can also connect you with [lead investor name] for a quote or background.]

Would this fit your editorial calendar?

[Your name]
[Title] | [Company]
[Phone]
```

## Part 4: Follow-Up Sequence

### After Sending the Pitch

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Send pitch |
| Day 3 | One follow-up (if no response): "Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried — happy to share more detail or schedule a call." |
| Day 7 | If still no response: Move on. Two follow-ups max. Journalists who don't respond are not interested — a third follow-up will get you blocked. |

**Note:** If a journalist responds with interest, respond within 2 hours regardless of time. Journalists work on tight deadlines.

## Part 5: Embargo Management

If you want to offer exclusives or embargoed announcements:

**Embargo process:**

1. **Offer exclusive first:** Contact your top-choice journalist and offer a 48-hour exclusive. If they decline or don't respond in 24 hours, offer to the next.

2. **Embargo timeline:**
   - Funding rounds: 1–2 weeks ahead is standard
   - Research: 5–7 days ahead
   - Product launch: 3–5 days ahead

3. **Embargo email language:**
   ```
   This information is embargoed until [DATE, TIME, TIMEZONE]. Please do not publish, post, or share before the embargo lifts. I'll confirm the embargo is lifted at [TIME] on [DATE].
   ```

4. **If embargo is broken:** Immediately release the information publicly (social + blog post + all journalists). The exclusivity is gone — release the full story.

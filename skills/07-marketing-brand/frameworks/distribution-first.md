# Distribution-First Framework

> The 80/20 principle of content: spend 20% of your effort creating and 80% distributing. Most startups do the opposite and wonder why content marketing doesn't work.

---

## The Core Insight

Every piece of content has a maximum potential audience — the people who would find it valuable if they saw it. Most content reaches 1–5% of that potential audience because distribution is an afterthought.

**The math:**
- A great piece with no distribution: 50–200 reads
- An average piece with excellent distribution: 5,000–50,000 reads
- A great piece with excellent distribution: compounding audience forever

**The implication:** Distribution is a higher-leverage activity than creation. Write fewer pieces. Distribute every piece 10× more.

## Distribution Tier Model

Not all distribution is equal. Organize channels by leverage and ownership:

### Tier 1: Owned (Highest Leverage)

You own these channels. No algorithm can take them away. Build these first.

| Channel | Why it's Tier 1 | How to build it |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Email list | Direct access to subscribers; no algorithm; opens are yours | Lead magnets, newsletter opt-in, gating high-value content |
| SMS list | Highest open rates (98%); use sparingly | Similar to email; requires explicit opt-in |
| Community (Slack/Discord) | Engaged audience you can message directly | Value-first content; takes 6–12 months to build |
| Podcast RSS feed | Subscribers are yours | Consistency and distribution to podcast apps |

**Priority rule:** Always include at least one Tier 1 channel in your distribution plan. If you have no Tier 1 audience, build one before investing heavily in Tier 2.

### Tier 2: Earned (High Leverage, Takes Time)

You don't own these but can earn access through relationships and quality.

| Channel | Why it's Tier 2 | How to earn access |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Newsletter inclusions/mentions | Others' lists; direct to engaged audience | Relationship + relevant content; pitch newsletter editors |
| Journalist coverage / PR | Large reach, high credibility | Newsworthy story + journalist relationship |
| Podcast appearances | Existing loyal audience | Pitch show hosts; be a remarkable guest |
| Co-promotion with complementary brands | Shared audience; no competition | Mutual value exchange |
| Guest posts | Other sites' SEO authority + readership | Pitch editors with strong angle + track record |

### Tier 3: Borrowed (Medium Leverage, Algorithm-Dependent)

You can use these immediately but don't own the audience. Algorithms can reduce reach anytime.

| Channel | Platform | Leverage | Owned alternative |
|---------|---------|---------|------------------|
| LinkedIn posts | LinkedIn | Medium-High | LinkedIn Newsletter (subscriber-based) |
| Twitter/X threads | Twitter/X | Medium | Newsletter |
| Instagram / TikTok | Meta / TikTok | Medium | Email list |
| YouTube | YouTube | Medium-High | RSS subscribers |
| Reddit / Hacker News | Reddit / HN | Variable | Own community |

**Rule:** Tier 3 channels should always point to Tier 1 assets. Use social to grow your email list, not as your primary owned asset.

## The 80/20 Distribution System

### Planning Phase (Before Writing)

Before you write a single word, answer:

1. **Who is the primary audience?** [Specific job title + company stage + situation]
2. **Where do they already consume content?** [Name specific channels, communities, newsletters]
3. **Who has already built an audience with them?** [Name specific people, newsletters, podcasts that reach them]
4. **What's the hook for distribution?** [Why will people share/mention this piece?]
5. **What's the call to action?** [What do you want readers to do after reading?]

**Distribution plan first, then create.** If you can't answer these questions, the topic may not be right for your content strategy.

### Creation Phase (20% of Total Time)

With your distribution plan in place, create the cornerstone content. Built-in distribution optimization:

- **Include data or statistics** — data-driven pieces get shared by journalists and newsletter writers at 3× the rate of opinion pieces
- **Quote or mention others** — they will share when they see the mention
- **Make it definitive** — comprehensive, well-researched pieces rank in search and get referenced repeatedly
- **Include a hook that works out of context** — the headline and first paragraph should work as a standalone tweet or LinkedIn post

### Distribution Phase (80% of Total Time)

Execute the distribution plan within 72 hours of publishing. Momentum decays quickly.

**Day 0 (Publish day):**

- [ ] Publish on primary channel
- [ ] Send to email list (high priority — Tier 1 first)
- [ ] Post on primary social channel with personalized introduction (not just link)
- [ ] Tag 2–3 people mentioned in the piece (they'll often reshare)
- [ ] Share in your Slack/Discord community if you have one

**Day 1–3:**

- [ ] Share in 3–5 relevant online communities (with proper context — not spam)
- [ ] Send to 2–3 newsletter partners you've pre-built relationships with
- [ ] Post on secondary social channels (reframe for each platform's format)
- [ ] Respond to every comment on the primary channel post (algorithms reward engagement)
- [ ] Post derivative content (thread, quote card, short video)

**Week 2:**

- [ ] Pitch the piece to 2 journalists if it has a newsworthy angle
- [ ] Publish syndication versions on Medium / Dev.to / Hashnode (with canonical link)
- [ ] Post the second derivative piece (different angle from week 1)
- [ ] Share in any pending newsletters that agreed to include it

**Month 2–6:**

- [ ] Update and republish if content is evergreen (SEO refresh)
- [ ] Reference in new related content (internal linking)
- [ ] Use as the basis for a future cornerstone on the same topic with newer data

## Community Distribution Rules

Communities are high-leverage but require respect. Violating these rules gets you banned.

### The 4:1 Rule for Community Sharing

For every piece of self-promotional content you share in a community, you must contribute 4 pieces of value with no self-promotion. Contribution can be:
- Answering someone's question thoroughly
- Sharing a resource from someone else
- Starting a discussion that generates value for members
- Giving feedback on someone's work

### Community Research Process

Before sharing in a community:
1. Read the rules. If it says "no self-promotion," take them at their word.
2. Spend 10 minutes engaging with existing discussions before posting.
3. Personalize the framing for the community's specific context. Generic shares ("check out my post") fail.
4. If allowed: lead with the value of the piece, not the link.

### Community Distribution Template

```
[Community-specific context: why this is relevant to THIS community specifically]

[What you found / what the piece teaches — 2–3 sentences of value with no link required to get the value]

[Optional: "I wrote about this in depth if you want to go deeper:" + link]
```

## The Newsletter Pitch System

Newsletter partnerships are one of the highest-ROI distribution channels — especially for reaching niche, engaged audiences.

### Finding Newsletters to Pitch

Sources:
- **Substack:** Search by topic category
- **Beehiiv:** Search by topic
- **Newsletter databases:** Newsletter.co, SparkLoop, MentionMe
- **Manual:** Google "[your topic] newsletter" or "[ICP job title] newsletter"

Qualification criteria:
- Covers topics your ICP cares about
- Has an engaged audience (open rate > 30% if disclosed)
- Is NOT a direct competitor's newsletter
- The editor is responsive (check their Twitter/LinkedIn activity)

### Newsletter Pitch Template

```
Subject: Pitch for [Newsletter Name] — [your piece title]

Hi [Editor name],

I'm a regular reader of [specific thing you appreciated about a recent issue].

I published [piece title] this week — [1 sentence summary of the value + the counterintuitive finding].

Key takeaway for your readers: [2–3 sentences of the most valuable insight, written in the newsletter's voice/style].

Full piece: [URL]

Happy to provide a custom excerpt or adapt the framing if it would be a better fit. No obligation either way.

[Your name]
```

**Success rate:** 10–20% when personalized. < 2% when generic.

## SEO as Distribution

SEO is a long-term distribution channel that compounds over time. Key rules for startups:

### The Startup SEO Hierarchy

1. **Long-tail, high-intent first:** "[specific problem] solution" or "how to [specific task]" — easier to rank, buyers are further down the funnel
2. **Build topical authority:** Write multiple comprehensive pieces on related subtopics before targeting the head term
3. **Internal linking:** Every new piece links to related pieces — distributes authority and helps readers discover more
4. **Update over creating new:** Refreshing existing ranked content is often more effective than creating new pieces for the same keyword

### Minimum SEO Setup

- [ ] Google Search Console: submit sitemap, verify domain
- [ ] One tracking pixel: Google Analytics 4 or equivalent
- [ ] Every page: unique title tag (< 60 chars), unique meta description (< 160 chars)
- [ ] Images: compressed (< 100KB for blog images), with descriptive alt text

## Distribution Metrics to Track

| Metric | What it measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Reach per piece | Total unique views across all channels | Improving trend over time |
| Email list growth from content | Direct conversions from content to list | > 1% of total readers |
| Referral traffic | Traffic from distribution channels (other sites, newsletters) | Growing month-over-month |
| Content-sourced leads | Leads who first engaged through content | Track and attribute in CRM |
| Inbound link growth | SEO authority building | 2–5 new referring domains per piece |

**The only distribution metric that matters for business:** Content-sourced leads or revenue attributed to content. Everything else is directional.

## Anti-Patterns in Distribution

| Anti-pattern | What it looks like | Fix |
|-------------|-------------------|-----|
| Fire-and-forget | Post once, move on | Use the distribution checklist; distribute for 72 hours minimum |
| Platform-dependent only | All followers are on LinkedIn, no email list | Convert 5–10% of followers to email subscribers every month |
| Spam in communities | Post links without context in 20 communities | 4:1 rule; personalized context; quality over quantity |
| Distribution after the fact | Write first, think about distribution later | Write the distribution plan before writing the piece |
| No follow-up | Great piece, no outreach to newsletter writers | Build newsletter relationship list; pitch for every piece |

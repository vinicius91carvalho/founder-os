# 60-Minute Validation Checklist

> A structured desk-research protocol to assess whether an idea is worth customer interviews — before investing time in scheduling and conducting conversations. Based on Tom Bilyeu's rapid validation approach. Complete in a single focused sitting.

---

## How to Use This Template

1. Fill in the idea and customer archetype at the top
2. Work through all 5 sections in sequence — each takes approximately 10–15 minutes
3. Score each section honestly using the rubric provided
4. Read the summary verdict and decide whether to proceed to customer interviews

**Total time:** 45–75 minutes
**Required tools:** Google (or Bing), Reddit, G2 / Trustpilot / App Store, Google Keyword Planner or Ahrefs (free tier), LinkedIn

## Section 4: Workaround and Adoption Signal Check (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Find evidence that people are cobbling together their own solutions — the strongest proof that the problem is real and no existing solution is satisfying.

**Where to look:**
- Reddit: "[problem keyword] + workaround", "[problem keyword] + how I solved", "[competitor name] + alternative"
- Indie Hackers: Search for founders who built something similar and read their journey posts
- Product Hunt: Search for products in this category — look at comments, not just the product pages
- GitHub: Look for open-source projects solving this problem — stars and forks indicate adoption
- Zapier/Make templates: Are there automation templates people have built? Template popularity = adoption signal

## NPS (Net Promoter Score)

### What NPS Measures

NPS measures loyalty and referral likelihood. It answers: "Would your customers tell others to buy from you?"

**The formula:**
```
NPS = % Promoters − % Detractors

Promoters: Scores 9–10 (enthusiastic, loyal, will refer)
Passives:  Scores 7–8 (satisfied but not enthusiastic; vulnerable to competition)
Detractors: Scores 0–6 (unhappy; could damage reputation through negative word-of-mouth)
```

**Benchmarks by industry:**
| Industry | Poor | Acceptable | Good | Excellent |
|---------|------|-----------|------|-----------|
| B2B SaaS | < 0 | 0–20 | 20–40 | > 40 |
| Consumer Software | < 0 | 0–30 | 30–50 | > 50 |
| Professional Services | < 10 | 10–30 | 30–50 | > 50 |

### NPS Survey Template

**Delivery method:** Email, in-product, or SMS
**Frequency:** Quarterly (or at major lifecycle milestones: post-onboarding, pre-renewal)
**Best send time:** Mid-week (Tuesday–Thursday), avoid Mondays and Fridays
**Response rate target:** 25–40% (below 15% = survey fatigue or bad timing)

### NPS Closed-Loop Action Protocol

**Rule:** Every NPS response receives a follow-up action within the defined SLA. No exceptions.

| Score | Segment | SLA | Action | Owner |
|-------|---------|-----|--------|-------|
| 0–4 | High-risk detractor | 24 hours | Phone call from CS manager or founder. Goal: understand root cause; create recovery plan. This is a retention emergency. | CS Manager / Founder |
| 5–6 | Detractor | 48 hours | Email from CS rep asking for a call to learn more. "Would you be open to 20 minutes so I can understand what happened and see if we can make it right?" | CS Rep |
| 7–8 | Passive | 5 business days | Email asking: "What would it take to move to a 9?" Focus on specific gaps. Do NOT pitch new features — listen. | CS Rep |
| 9–10 | Promoter | 5 business days | Thank you + ask: (1) testimonial or case study, (2) referral intro to a colleague with the same problem, (3) G2/Capterra/Trustpilot review. | CS Rep |

**Recovery protocol for detractors (0–6):**
1. Acknowledge the score immediately (same day): "I saw your [score] and I want to understand what's going wrong."
2. Listen first: "Can you tell me more about your experience? What specifically has been disappointing?"
3. Own the problem: Do not explain, justify, or defend. Acknowledge and commit to resolution.
4. Create a recovery plan with specific commitments: "Here's what I'm going to do by [date]."
5. Follow up: Did the committed actions happen? Did the customer's experience improve?
6. Re-survey in 60 days to check if score improved.

## CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score)

### What CSAT Measures

CSAT measures satisfaction with a specific interaction or moment — it's transaction-level feedback, not relationship-level. Best used after: support interactions, onboarding calls, feature releases, QBRs.

**The formula:**
```
CSAT = (Positive responses / Total responses) × 100

Positive = "Satisfied" or "Very Satisfied" (4–5 on a 5-point scale)
Target: ≥ 80% for support interactions
```

## Survey Program Operations

### Response Rate Optimization

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| Length | Maximum 3 questions. Every additional question reduces response rate by ~10%. |
| Timing | Send within 24 hours of the trigger event. Delayed surveys lose context. |
| Personalization | Use first name; send from a real person's email (not noreply@); include context |
| Mobile optimization | 60%+ of survey responses come from mobile. Test on mobile before sending. |
| Frequency | Don't survey the same customer more than once per quarter. Survey fatigue is real. |
| A/B test subject lines | Test 2 subject lines on first 20% of sends; send the winner to the rest |

### NPS Program Calendar

| Month | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| Month 1 of quarter | Send NPS survey to all active customers |
| Weeks 1–2 | Close the loop: all responses actioned per SLA above |
| Week 3–4 | Compile results; segment by timing, ARR, industry |
| End of quarter | Report: overall NPS, promoter/passive/detractor breakdown, top themes, actions taken |
| Board meeting | Include NPS trend in board deck (1 slide, with specific closed-loop examples) |

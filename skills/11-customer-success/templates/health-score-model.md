# Customer Health Score Model

> **Purpose:** Build a composite health score (0–100) that aggregates multiple customer signals into a single number predicting renewal likelihood. Use it to prioritize CS attention and trigger interventions before customers decide to leave.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This template was generated by an AI system. Health score weights should be validated against your actual churn data — intuitive weights are a starting point, not a final answer. Your specific product, customer segment, and business model will require custom calibration.

---

## Health Score Design Principles

1. **Fewer, more predictive signals > many weak signals.** Start with 4–6 signals. Adding more doesn't improve accuracy if the additional signals aren't correlated with churn in your data.
2. **Use leading indicators, not lagging ones.** "Customer has churned" is a lagging indicator. "Customer's weekly active users dropped 40% in 30 days" is a leading indicator.
3. **All signals must be measurable without manual input.** If calculating a signal requires a CS rep to manually enter data, it will be wrong or stale.
4. **Validate, then adjust.** After 6–12 months, correlate historical health scores with actual renewal outcomes. Adjust weights based on what actually predicted churn.

### Signal 1: Product Usage Frequency

**What it measures:** How actively customers use the product (not just that they're logged in)

**Why it predicts churn:** Customers who aren't using the product have no switching cost and are making an implicit decision to leave.

**How to measure:**
- **SaaS products:** Weekly Active Users (WAU) as % of licensed seats
- **Usage platforms:** Actions taken / events fired per user per week
- **Content tools:** Content created or consumed per week per active user

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | Threshold | Label |

### Signal 2: Feature Adoption Depth

**What it measures:** How many of the core features a customer has activated and uses regularly

**Why it predicts churn:** Customers who only use 1–2 features have lower switching costs and derive less value from the product. Deeper feature adoption = higher stickiness.

**How to measure:**
- Define your "core feature set" (the 5–10 features that deliver the core value proposition)
- Track % of features each customer has used at least once in the last 30 days
- Weight features by their correlation with retention (power features > peripheral features)

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | Threshold | Label |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| 100 | ≥ 80% of core features used in last 30 days | Power user |
| 80 | 60–79% of core features | Active adopter |
| 60 | 40–59% of core features | Partial adopter |
| 40 | 20–39% of core features | Shallow adopter |
| 20 | < 20% of core features | At-risk |

### Signal 3: Support Ticket Volume and Sentiment

**What it measures:** Volume of support requests and whether they indicate frustration vs. growth

**Why it predicts churn:** High ticket volume can signal problems (customer is struggling, product is failing them) OR growth (customer is expanding). Differentiate: frustration tickets (bugs, "this doesn't work") are negative signals; feature request tickets can be positive.

**How to measure:**
- Track: tickets per user per month, ticket type (bug vs. question vs. feature request), time-to-resolution, customer satisfaction rating per ticket
- Sentiment: categorize tickets as Frustrated / Neutral / Positive

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | Threshold | Label |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| 100 | Low volume, high CSAT (≥ 4.5/5), mostly questions | Healthy |
| 80 | Moderate volume, CSAT 4–4.5 | Acceptable |
| 60 | High volume OR CSAT 3.5–4 | Concerning |
| 40 | High volume AND CSAT < 3.5 | At-risk |
| 20 | Escalations, repeated same bug, CSAT < 3 | Critical |

**Default weight:** 15% of total score

### Signal 4: NPS / Satisfaction Score

**What it measures:** How likely a customer is to recommend you (NPS) or how satisfied they are (CSAT)

**Why it predicts churn:** Promoters (NPS 9–10) churn at much lower rates than detractors (NPS 0–6). Direct correlation between stated satisfaction and renewal behavior.

**How to measure:**
- NPS: ask quarterly — "How likely are you to recommend [Product] to a colleague?"
- CSAT: ask post-onboarding and quarterly — "How satisfied are you with [Product]?"

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | NPS | CSAT | Label |
|-------|-----|------|-------|
| 100 | 9–10 | 4.5–5 | Promoter |
| 80 | 7–8 | 4–4.5 | Passive (satisfied) |
| 50 | 5–6 | 3–4 | Mixed |
| 20 | 3–4 | 2–3 | Detractor |
| 0 | 0–2 | < 2 | At-risk |

**Default weight:** 20% of total score

### Signal 5: Executive / Champion Engagement

**What it measures:** Whether the customer's decision-maker (not just the day-to-day user) is engaged with the product and the relationship

**Why it predicts churn:** The most common cause of unexpected churn is champion departure (the person who championed the purchase leaves). If the executive layer is engaged, the product is harder to cancel even when champions change.

**How to measure (primarily manual, make it a CS habit):**
- Track: last executive sponsor call date, attendance at QBRs, responsiveness to CS outreach
- Define: "executive sponsor" = person with budget authority for the product

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | Threshold | Label |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| 100 | Executive engaged in last 30 days; attends QBRs | Engaged |
| 80 | Executive engaged in last 60 days | Connected |
| 50 | Executive engaged in last 90 days | Passive |
| 20 | Executive last engaged > 90 days ago | Disconnected |
| 0 | Executive sponsor has departed or is unknown | Critical |

**Default weight:** 10% of total score

### Signal 6: Billing Health

**What it measures:** Whether the customer pays on time and without dispute

**Why it predicts churn:** Customers who dispute invoices or consistently pay late have lower commitment to the product. Pending payment failures are often the first administrative signal before a formal cancellation request.

**How to measure:**
- Track: days past due on most recent invoice, dispute history, failed payment attempts

**Healthy / Warning / At-Risk thresholds:**

| Score | Threshold | Label |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| 100 | Always pays on time, no disputes | Excellent |
| 80 | Occasionally late (< 15 days), no disputes | Acceptable |
| 50 | Frequently late (15–30 days) OR 1 dispute in 12 months | Concerning |
| 20 | Consistently late (> 30 days) OR active dispute | At-risk |
| 0 | Overdue > 60 days OR multiple disputes | Critical |

**Default weight:** 5% of total score

# Churn Analysis Template — Segmented by Timing and Reason

> **Purpose:** Analyze churned customers systematically to identify root causes by timing segment. The most important insight: churn at different lifecycle stages requires different interventions. This template makes that segmentation explicit.

---

> **Disclaimer:** This template was generated by an AI system. Churn analysis is most valuable when based on direct customer interviews, not just internal data. The most important data in this template comes from talking to churned customers directly.

---

## The Core Insight: Timing Changes Everything

Treating all churn the same is the most common CS anti-pattern. Before asking "why," ask "when."

| When They Churned | Root Cause is Usually | Intervention Category |
|------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| **0–30 days** | Onboarding failure, complexity, wrong ICP sold | Onboarding redesign, pre-sales qualification |
| **31–180 days** | Adoption failure, champion lost internal buy-in | Adoption playbook, executive engagement |
| **6–18 months** | ROI failure, can't prove value, budget pressure | Value delivery, business review process |
| **18+ months** | Competitive displacement, strategic shift | Competitive response, strategic account planning |

*Run this analysis quarterly. After each analysis, identify the 1–2 highest-leverage changes.*

## Part 4: Exit Interview Findings

*Exit interviews are the highest-signal data in this document. Direct quotes from churned customers beat any analytics.*

**Exit interview methodology:**
- Who conducts: CS manager or founder (not the CS rep who owned the account — removes awkwardness)
- Format: 20-minute video call, or 5-question email survey if call declined
- Timing: Within 14 days of cancellation
- Incentive: $25–50 gift card for their time

**Exit interview questions:**
1. What was the primary reason you decided to cancel?
2. Was there a specific moment when you decided to cancel, or was it a gradual decision?
3. What would have needed to be different for you to stay?
4. What are you doing instead — did you choose a competitor, or are you solving this differently?
5. Is there anything we could have done as a CS team to change the outcome?

# Time-to-First-Value (TTFV) Methodology

> **Purpose:** A systematic approach to defining, measuring, and reducing the time between "contract signed" and "customer first experiences the core value of your product." TTFV is the single most actionable leading indicator of long-term customer retention.

> **Disclaimer:** This framework was generated by an AI system using established CS methodology (Lincoln Murphy, Gainsight). TTFV definitions and targets are highly product-specific — this framework provides the methodology; you must calibrate it to your product and customer data.

## Why TTFV Is the Most Important CS Metric

Most CS metrics are lagging indicators — they tell you what happened (churn rate, NPS, expansion revenue). TTFV is a leading indicator: it predicts what will happen.

**The research is consistent across SaaS companies:**
- Customers who reach first value within the target window retain at materially higher rates
- Every day of delay between signing and first value is a day the customer is paying for a promise, not an outcome
- The longer it takes to reach first value, the more buyer's remorse accumulates

**TTFV also drives every other CS metric:**
- Fast TTFV → Higher early-period health scores
- Fast TTFV → Better onboarding NPS
- Fast TTFV → Lower 30-day and 90-day churn
- Fast TTFV → Higher expansion rates (customers who got value fast expand faster)

## Phase 1: Define First Value

**The definition must be:**
1. **Specific** — Not "customer is happy" but "customer completed [specific action] and saw [specific outcome]"
2. **Measurable** — You can track this event in your product analytics
3. **Customer-validated** — Real customers confirm this moment felt valuable, not what you assume feels valuable

### How to Find Your First Value Definition

**Step 1: Interview your best customers (10–15 customers)**

Ask them:
- "When did you first feel like [Product Name] was going to work for you?"
- "Was there a specific moment or was it gradual?"
- "What had you done right before that moment?"
- "Before that moment, were you worried it might not work out?"

Listen for the specific moment they describe. It will likely cluster around one or two events.

**Step 2: Map the events in your product**

Once you have the descriptions, find the corresponding events in your analytics:
- "When I first imported all my data" → `data_import_completed` event
- "When my whole team was using it, not just me" → `second_user_completed_first_task` event
- "When I saw the first automated report" → `first_scheduled_report_delivered` event

**Step 3: Validate with churn correlation**

Pull the event timing data for all your customers. Compare:
- Customers who triggered the event within [X days]: what is their 6-month retention rate?
- Customers who took longer or never triggered it: what is their 6-month retention rate?

If the retention difference is significant (typically 20–40 percentage points), you've found the right event.

## Phase 4: Reduce TTFV

### Intervention Categories

| Intervention Type | TTFV Reduction | Effort | Example |
|------------------|---------------|--------|---------|
| Remove a step | High | Medium | If data import is blocking, build an automatic import from the most common source |
